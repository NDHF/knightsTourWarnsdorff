The Knight's Tour has challenged chess players and mathematicians for over 1,000 years.

The goal is to move make the knight touch every square on the board once, and only once.

In 1823, mathematician H.C. Warnsdorff published a strategy for completing the challenge: Move the knight to the square which offers the smallest number of moves.

This strategy, known as the Warnsdorff Heuristic, is not the most optimal strategy, and is only a general guide.
